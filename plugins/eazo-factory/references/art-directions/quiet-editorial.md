# Art direction: quiet-editorial

Palette tendencies:
- Soft paper neutrals, ink black, muted moss, weathered bronze, and one low-saturation accent.
- Contrast should feel calm and legible rather than glossy.

Composition:
- Editorial whitespace, anchored text blocks, and one restrained visual focal area.
- Prefer calm vertical flow and clear grouping over ornamental layering.

Typography:
- Refined serif for display paired with unobtrusive sans serif for interface copy.
- Copy should feel measured, literary, and easy to scan.

Imagery:
- Minimal still-life abstractions, atmospheric gradients, silhouettes, and quietly textured surfaces.
- Imagery supports mood and hierarchy, not novelty.

Motion:
- Fades, measured lifts, soft masks, and low-amplitude breathing motion.
- Motion should reinforce calm pacing and orientation.

Forbidden clichés:
- Luxury clichés, glossy glassmorphism, loud neon accents, stock-lifestyle hero shots, fake magazine covers.

UI-control discipline:
- Keep visible controls sparse and necessary.
- Primary actions should be obvious through placement and contrast, not size inflation.
- Any ornamental object that reads like a tappable pill, card, or chip must either become a mapped control or be removed.
