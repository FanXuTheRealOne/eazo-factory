# Art direction: matisse-cut-paper

Palette tendencies:
- Sun-washed cream, marine blue, leaf green, terracotta, charcoal.
- Large flat color fields with one vivid accent at a time.

Composition:
- Original cut-paper composition built from bold organic shapes and confident negative space.
- Use asymmetry, overlap, and rhythm; do not copy any existing artwork or composition.
- Favor 2-4 major forms per screen over dense decorative fragments.

Typography:
- Simple, sturdy sans serif or lightly humanist serif.
- Large headings, restrained body copy, generous margins.

Imagery:
- Abstract leaves, waves, breath arcs, or body-adjacent silhouettes reduced to paper-like forms.
- Textures may suggest cut edges or layered paper, but remain subtle and reproducible.

Motion:
- Slow sliding layers, gentle reveals, and soft parallax.
- Motion should feel buoyant, never twitchy or elastic.

Forbidden clichés:
- Literal museum pastiche, faux canvas cracks, obvious collage clutter, posterized portraits.
- Any direct imitation of a known Matisse artwork.

UI-control discipline:
- Controls must remain clearly separate from decorative paper forms.
- One dominant action per state; supporting controls should be visually quieter and minimal.
- Decorative shapes must never look tappable unless they map to a real interaction entry.
